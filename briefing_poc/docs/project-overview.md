# Project Overview

## What This Is

An automated pipeline that generates comprehensive briefing documents for newly elected municipal officials. Given a city name, it collects legislative records, budget data, constituent voter modeling, and local news coverage, then uses LLM analysis to produce an actionable briefing.

The product vision is a **conversational onboarding experience** — not a static PDF. An elected official walks through a guided flow and can ask the AI to go deeper at any point.

### The CPO's 6-Step Flow

1. **Constituency deep dive** — who are your voters?
2. **Key issues facing your constituents** — what do they care about?
3. **Council time vs. constituent priorities** — where are the mismatches?
4. **Top budget challenges** — what financial pressures should you understand?
5. **Quick wins with CTAs for polling** — what can you do right now?
6. **Longer term governance plan** — how do you make progress on your platform?

## What We Proved (3 Cities)

The pipeline has been validated end-to-end for 3 NC cities, each testing a different dimension:

| City | Legislative System | Matters | Voters | Discussions | Quick Wins | Briefing Size | LLM Cost |
|------|-------------------|---------|--------|-------------|------------|---------------|----------|
| Charlotte | Legistar (REST API) | 686 | 642,783 | 19 items, 145 sources | 14 actions | 73,214 chars | $0.014 |
| Wake County | Legistar (REST API) | 272 | 800,550 | 6 items, 42 sources | 13 actions | 56,935 chars | $0.009 |
| Raleigh | eSCRIBE (JSON API) | 441 | 379,996 | 9 items, 91 sources | 10 actions | 50,300 chars | $0.009 |

### Key Findings

- **Multi-city generalization works.** The pipeline produces useful briefings for different entity types (City Council vs. Board of Commissioners), different legislative systems, and different data volumes.
- **Pluggable collector architecture validated.** Three very different integration patterns (REST API, AJAX scraping, JSON API) all produce identical JSON output. Downstream scripts work unchanged.
- **Prompt tokens stay manageable.** Charlotte ~230K, Wake County ~182K, Raleigh ~104K — all under Gemini's 1M-token context window. Vector search is not needed.
- **Haystaq voter columns are consistent across NC.** Same 15 issue score columns for all NC cities/counties. Per-city topic mapping is auto-generated by script 06b.
- **NC LINC budget API works for any NC municipality.** Filter by `area_name` and get 44 years of fiscal data.
- **Adding a city on an existing platform takes ~3 hours.** Most of that is research and config. Pipeline runtime is ~30-60 minutes.
- **Building a new collector takes 1-2 days.** The output schema is locked in and the pattern is established.

## Key Decisions Still Needed

1. **Conversational flow vs. downloadable PDFs?** The CPO says PDF is temporary, but some officials might prefer a document. Can we support both from the same section-level JSON?

2. **How personalized does it need to be?** The pipeline generates city-wide briefings. How much do we know about the official when they start? Do they self-select role/district, or do we pre-populate from GoodParty candidate data?

3. **Can we launch without discussion narratives?** Meeting minutes and news coverage are the hardest data to collect at scale. The POC proves useful briefings without them.

4. **Where does the conversational AI live?** Options: (a) built into gp-webapp with Claude API, (b) standalone tool, (c) embedded chat sidebar in the onboarding flow.

5. **What triggers briefing generation?** Options: (a) GoodParty staff kicks it off, (b) official signs up and it auto-generates, (c) scheduled refresh after each meeting.
